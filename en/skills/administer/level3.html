Level definition

Level 3 of the ADMINISTER competency corresponds to advanced administration and security. This competency is part of the 6 competencies of the BUT Computer Science program and represents the third level of learning (Critical Learning AC33), the most advanced level. At this level, I am able to participate in the design and implementation of information system (IS) projects, measure impacts (economic, societal, environmental), and adapt an information system to organizational needs.

This level represents my ability to manage complex production infrastructures, ensure system security, and guarantee high service availability. These skills are developed mainly during semesters 5 and 6 (third year of BUT Computer Science) through educational resources R4.09, R3.09, R5.09 and R5.07, as well as academic projects SAÉ 5.01 and my professional work-study experience at Agence Altimax.

Status: IN PROGRESS

Resources at this level are currently being studied and competencies are being developed through academic projects and professional work-study experience.

Critical Learning (AC) for Level 3

Critical Learning (AC) are the essential elements I must master to achieve this competency level. Here are the 3 critical learning items for Level 3:

AC33.01
Participate in the design and implementation of an information system project
Process automation (automation of repetitive tasks), configuration management (management of system parameters), CI/CD (Continuous Integration / Continuous Deployment), orchestration (automated management of multiple services), information system design (architecture of an organization's information systems)
AC33.02
Measure impacts (economic, societal, environmental)
Impact evaluation (analysis of consequences), economic analysis (costs, benefits), societal (impact on society) and environmental (impact on the environment), consequence measurement
AC33.03
Adapt an information system to organizational needs
System adaptation (modification according to needs), evolution according to needs (continuous evolution), digital transformation (digital modernization), business alignment (correspondence with business needs)

Traces and contextualized evidence

Traces are raw documents that testify to my activity. Evidence are these traces contextualized with reflection on learning. Here are my traces and evidence for Level 3:

EVIDENCE 1: SAÉ 5.01 - SmartHome (IoT architecture, MQTT security, virtualization)

Context: Academic project carried out in the third year (Semester 5) as part of SAÉ 5.01. This is a complex project developing an Android mobile application with Kotlin, integrating IoT (Internet of Things) via MQTT and Raspberry Pi, and using artificial intelligence, requiring the design and implementation of a complete and secure information system.

Trace:

  • Complete information system architecture with mapping (R4.09)
  • MQTT communication security (IoT protocol) with cryptography (R3.09)
  • Secure IoT architecture for data collection and processing
  • Information system architecture documentation
  • Application source code with secure architecture (project in progress, confidential)

Justification: This project allowed me to learn to participate in the design and implementation of an information system (AC33.01) by creating a complete architecture with a mapping that showed how everything was organized. I also discovered the importance of securing systems by protecting MQTT communication with cryptography so that data is well protected. I used knowledge from resources R4.09 (Advanced management of information systems, mapping) and R3.09 (Cryptography and security).

Reflection: This project is the most complex in terms of system administration as it combines several technologies (mobile, IoT, AI) requiring a sophisticated information system architecture. The main difficulty was designing a secure architecture for IoT while guaranteeing performance and reliability. I overcame this difficulty by studying secure IoT architectures, applying cryptography concepts, and using methods taught in resources R4.09 and R3.09. This project showed me the importance of designing information system architectures for complex projects.

EVIDENCE 2: WORK-STUDY - Agence Altimax (WordPress system administration)

Context: Professional work-study since September 2025 (4 months, in progress) as a Web Developer at Agence Altimax in Annecy. I develop and maintain websites with WordPress, requiring system administration, environment management, and adaptation of the information system to organizational needs.

Trace:

  • WordPress system administration in production
  • Environment management (development, staging, production)
  • Adaptation of the information system to organizational needs
  • WordPress websites in production (proprietary, not public)
  • Regular contributions with documented Git commits
  • System administration documentation

Justification: This work-study allowed me to learn to participate in the design and implementation of an information system (AC33.01) in a real professional context, by administering the company's WordPress systems. I discovered how to manage these systems on a daily basis. I also learned to adapt an information system to organizational needs (AC33.03) by adapting WordPress sites to the specific needs of each client. This daily experience allows me to apply my system administration skills in a real context with all professional constraints.

Reflection: This work-study is a continuous professional experience that allows me to develop my advanced system administration skills in a real context. The main difficulty was understanding how to administer and adapt WordPress systems according to client needs while respecting company constraints. I overcame this difficulty by communicating regularly with the team, following company procedures, and applying system administration best practices. This experience showed me the importance of adapting information systems to organizational needs and allowed me to develop my ability to manage systems in production.
