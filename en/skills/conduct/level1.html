Level definition

Level 1 of the CONDUCT competency corresponds to simple project management. This competency represents the first level of learning (Critical Learning AC15). At this level, I am able to identify business needs of clients and users, set up project management tools, and identify stakeholders and project phases.

This level represents my first steps in project management, where I learned the fundamental concepts of planning and project tracking. These skills were developed during semesters 1 and 2 (first and second years of BUT Computer Science) through educational resources R2.10 (Management, accounting and Gantt), as well as academic projects SAÉ 2.05.

Critical Learning (AC) for Level 1

Here are the 3 critical learning items for Level 1:

AC15.01
Identify business needs of clients and users
Needs analysis (understanding expectations), understanding business requirements (professional needs), identifying objectives (defining project goals)
AC15.02
Set up project management tools
Tool selection and configuration (selecting appropriate tools), setting up management processes (work organization)
AC15.03
Identify stakeholders and project phases
Stakeholder identification (people involved), project phase definition (project steps), organization (project structuring)

Traces and contextualized evidence

Here are my traces and evidence for Level 1:

EVIDENCE 1: Specifications document

Context: Academic project carried out in the second year (Semester 2) as part of SAÉ 2.05. This was a system analysis and project management project for a maintenance and work management system for the city of Dortan. This project required the identification of business needs, the setup of project management tools, and the identification of stakeholders and project phases.

Trace:

  • Specifications document (document describing system needs and requirements)
  • Business needs analysis (identification of required functionalities)
  • UML diagrams (use cases, activity diagrams, sequence diagrams)
  • Project documentation with stakeholder and phase identification
  • Project management tools (planning, monitoring)
  • Documentation available on GitHub

Justification: This project taught me to identify business needs of clients and users (AC15.01) by analyzing the needs of the city of Dortan for the maintenance management system and documenting these needs in a structured specifications document. I also discovered how to set up project management tools (AC15.02) by organizing work with planning and monitoring tools to manage the analysis and project documentation. Finally, I learned to identify stakeholders and project phases (AC15.03) by documenting the different stakeholders involved (municipality, technical services, users) and project phases (analysis, specification, planning). I used resources R2.10 (Management, accounting and Gantt) and system analysis methods.

Reflection: This project was an enriching experience in project management and needs analysis. The main difficulty was understanding how to structure a complete specifications document and identify all business needs of a complex system. I overcame this difficulty by studying system analysis methods, creating UML diagrams to visualize needs, and applying project management concepts. This project showed me the importance of properly documenting needs in a structured specifications document and allowed me to develop my skills in identifying business needs.

Pedagogical reflection and progression
Challenges encountered
The main challenges at this level concerned understanding real user needs, structuring a project from its initial phase, and using project management tools.
Solutions provided
To overcome these difficulties, I studied needs analysis methods, created a structured specifications document, used UML diagrams to visualize needs, used planning tools like the Gantt chart, and applied methods taught in class.

Progression Level 1 allowed me to acquire the basics of project management. Through the SAÉ 2.05 project, I learned to identify business needs by creating a specifications document, set up project management tools with the Gantt chart, and identify stakeholders and project phases. These experiences allowed me to develop a solid foundation in project management, from understanding needs to work organization. This first experience showed me the importance of properly structuring a project from the start with a complete specifications document and prepared me for the following levels.
