Level definition

Level 3 of the CONDUCT competency corresponds to advanced project management. This competency is part of the 6 competencies of the BUT Computer Science program and represents the third level of learning (Critical Learning AC35), the most advanced level. At this level, I am able to measure project impacts (economic, societal, environmental), integrate a project into an information system, and adapt an information system to evolving needs.

This level represents my ability to take charge of strategic projects, establish processes, and guarantee project quality and continuity. These skills are developed mainly during semesters 5 and 6 (third year of BUT Computer Science) through educational resources R5.01, R4.09, R5.02 and R5.13, as well as academic projects SAÉ 5.01 and my professional work-study experience at Agence Altimax.

Status: IN PROGRESS

Resources at this level are currently being studied and competencies are being developed through academic projects and professional work-study experience.

Critical Learning (AC) for Level 3

Critical Learning (AC) are the essential elements I must master to achieve this competency level. Here are the 3 critical learning items for Level 3:

AC35.01
Measure project impacts
Evaluation of economic impacts (costs, benefits), societal (impact on society) and technological (technical impact), value analysis (value provided), results measurement
AC35.02
Integrate a project into an information system
Integration into information systems (connection with existing systems), IS architecture (organization of systems), compatibility (working with existing systems), business alignment (correspondence with business needs)
AC35.03
Adapt an information system to evolving needs
System evolution (continuous modification), adaptation to changing needs (flexibility), digital transformation (digital modernization)

Traces and contextualized evidence

Traces are raw documents that testify to my activity. Evidence are these traces contextualized with reflection on learning. Here are my traces and evidence for Level 3:

EVIDENCE 1: SAÉ 5.01 - SmartHome (Impact measurement, system integration, team management)

Context: Academic project carried out in the third year (Semester 5) as part of SAÉ 5.01. This is a complex project developing an Android mobile application with Kotlin, integrating IoT (Internet of Things) via MQTT and Raspberry Pi, and using artificial intelligence, requiring impact measurement, integration into an information system, and team management.

Trace:

  • Measurement of project impacts (economic, societal, environmental) via Digital Fresco (R5.13)
  • Integration of a project into an information system with mapping (R4.09)
  • Team management with RACI (responsibility matrix) and conflict management (R5.01)
  • Information system architecture documentation
  • Application source code (project in progress, confidential)

Justification: This project allowed me to learn to measure project impacts (AC35.01) by using the Digital Fresco to evaluate the economic, societal and environmental impacts of our work. I also discovered how to integrate a project into an information system (AC35.02) by creating a mapping that showed how everything was articulated and by integrating several technologies (mobile, IoT, AI). Finally, I developed my team management skills by using RACI to clarify roles and managing conflicts that could arise. I used resources R5.01 (Team management), R4.09 (IS Mapping) and R5.13 (Digital Fresco).

Reflection: This project is the most complex in terms of project management as it combines several aspects: impact measurement, system integration, and team management. The main difficulty was coordinating all these aspects while managing a team and measuring project impacts. I overcame this difficulty by applying team management methods (RACI), using mapping to visualize system integration, and applying Digital Fresco concepts to measure impacts. This project showed me the importance of a global vision in advanced project management.

EVIDENCE 2: WORK-STUDY - Agence Altimax (Project management, specification tracking, RACI)

Context: Professional work-study since September 2025 (4 months, in progress) as a Web Developer at Agence Altimax in Annecy. I participate in project management, specification tracking, use of RACI, impact measurement, integration of projects into the information system, and adaptation of the information system to organizational needs.

Trace:

  • Project management with specification tracking
  • Use of RACI (responsibility matrix) to clarify roles
  • Measurement of project impacts (economic, societal)
  • Integration of projects into the company's information system
  • Adaptation of the information system to changing client needs
  • WordPress websites in production (proprietary, not public)
  • Regular contributions with documented Git commits

Justification: This work-study allowed me to learn to measure project impacts (AC35.01) by evaluating the economic and societal impacts of the projects we developed. I discovered the importance of thinking about the consequences of our work. I also learned to integrate a project into an information system (AC35.02) by integrating WordPress sites into the company's information system so they work with the rest. Finally, I developed my ability to adapt an information system to evolving needs (AC35.03) by adapting sites to changing client needs. This daily experience allows me to apply my project management skills in a real context with all professional constraints.

Reflection: This work-study is a continuous professional experience that allows me to develop my advanced project management skills in a real context. The main difficulty was understanding how to measure project impacts in a professional context and how to adapt systems to changing client needs. I overcame this difficulty by communicating regularly with the team, using RACI to clarify responsibilities, and applying project management best practices. This experience showed me the importance of flexibility and adaptation in professional project management and allowed me to develop my ability to manage complex projects in production.
