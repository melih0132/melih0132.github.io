Level definition

Level 3 of the MANAGE competency corresponds to advanced data management and big data (management of very large amounts of data). This competency is part of the 6 competencies of the BUT Computer Science program and represents the third level of learning (Critical Learning AC34), the most advanced level. At this level, I am able to design data architectures (complex organization of data systems), use NoSQL technologies (non-relational databases), and manage data governance (rules and processes for managing data).

This level represents my ability to manage complex data architectures, work with different storage technologies, and ensure data governance. These skills are developed mainly during semesters 5 and 6 (third year of BUT Computer Science) through educational resources R4.03, R4.06, R5.10 and R4.09, as well as academic projects SAÉ 5.01 and my professional work-study experience at Agence Altimax.

Status: IN PROGRESS

Resources at this level are currently being studied and competencies are being developed through academic projects and professional work-study experience.

Critical Learning (AC) for Level 3

Critical Learning (AC) are the essential elements I must master to achieve this competency level. Here are the 3 critical learning items for Level 3:

AC34.01
Participate in the design and implementation of decision support systems
Big Data management (very large amounts of data), large-scale data storage, decision support systems (data-based decision support systems), storage architectures (organization of data storage systems)
AC34.02
Integrate complex data management solutions
ETL (Extract, Transform, Load - data preparation process), data preparation, extraction, transformation, loading, data pipelines (data processing flows), integration of complex solutions
AC34.03
Take into account regulatory issues (GDPR, compliance)
GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation - European data protection law), compliance (respect for rules), regulatory issues (legal aspects), data protection (security and confidentiality)

Traces and contextualized evidence

Traces are raw documents that testify to my activity. Evidence are these traces contextualized with reflection on learning. Here are my traces and evidence for Level 3:

EVIDENCE 1: SAÉ 5.01 - SmartHome (Advanced MongoDB, IoT data, AI classification)

Context: Academic project carried out in the third year (Semester 5) as part of SAÉ 5.01. This is a complex project developing an Android mobile application with Kotlin, integrating IoT (Internet of Things) via MQTT and Raspberry Pi, and using artificial intelligence for data classification, requiring advanced data management with MongoDB.

Trace:

  • Data architecture with advanced MongoDB to store IoT data
  • Decision support system to analyze collected data
  • Data visualization and infographics to present data (R4.06)
  • Information system mapping (R4.09)
  • Application source code with data management (project in progress, confidential)

Justification: This project allowed me to learn to design and implement decision support systems (AC34.01) by creating a system that analyzed all the IoT data we collected. I also discovered how to integrate complex data management solutions (AC34.02) by using advanced MongoDB to manage the large amounts of data coming from IoT. I used knowledge from resources R4.03 (MongoDB), R4.06 (Data visualization), R5.10 (Advanced MongoDB) and R4.09 (IS Mapping).

Reflection: This project is the most complex in terms of data management as it combines several data sources (IoT, AI, mobile) requiring a sophisticated data architecture. The main difficulty was designing a MongoDB architecture adapted to manage real-time IoT data while allowing analysis and classification by AI. I overcame this difficulty by studying data architectures for IoT, practicing with advanced MongoDB, and applying concepts taught in resources R4.03, R4.06, R5.10 and R4.09. This project showed me the importance of designing data architectures for complex systems.

I discovered the real challenges of data management in production. I also learned to take into account regulatory issues (AC34.03) by respecting GDPR in client data management, which is essential today. This daily experience allows me to apply my data management skills in a real context with all production and compliance constraints.

Reflection: This work-study is a continuous professional experience that allows me to develop my advanced data management skills in a real context. The main difficulty was understanding how to manage client data while respecting GDPR and optimizing performance. I overcame this difficulty by studying GDPR requirements, communicating with the team, and applying data management best practices. This experience showed me the importance of regulatory compliance in professional data management and allowed me to develop my ability to manage data in a production context.
